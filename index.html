Our Story

A Family Tradition Worth Savoring

Bluebird Café & Diner opened its doors in 1958 when Merle and Florence Peper decided Arlington deserved a place where families could gather over honest, soul-nourishing food. More than six decades later, we're still family-owned, still pouring bottomless coffee, and still perfecting those legendary blueberry pancakes.

Today the café is owned and run by their youngest daughter, Shirley Parris. Every plate that leaves our kitchen carries the same commitment to quality and warmth that Merle and Florence instilled from day one — generous portions, familiar flavors, and a dining room that feels like home from the moment you walk in.

BlueBird
Café & Diner
Since 1958

In 1958, Merle and Florence Peper opened up The Blue Bird Café for 24-hour services. Weekday mornings log trucks would line main street by 3am so Florence could fill their bellies and send them off to work with a sack lunch. The whole family was involved; The older kids would shred hash browns and grind beef in the basement of their home. Florence was so devoted she was still making her homemade biscuits into her early 70's. Today the café is owned and ran by their youngest daughter, Shirley Parris. Memories of Florence still fill these walls every day.
